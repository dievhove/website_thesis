Project Information

Honeybees (Apis mellifera) are crucial pollinators, supporting biodiversity and global food production. A third of the world’s food production depends on bees, with many crops like fruits, vegetables, and nuts reliant on their pollination. However, bee populations are under threat due to factors like habitat loss, pesticides, climate change, and disease. One of the most dangerous threats in Western Europe today is the Varroa destructor mite, a parasite that weakens honeybee colonies and spreads viruses, leading to colony collapse. Managing Varroa infestations has become a priority for beekeepers and researchers, with efforts focused on alternative control strategies such as breeding mite-resistant bees. The objective of this thesis is to develop a comprehensive data acquisition system to monitor honeybee colony health and behavior. This system integrates sensors connected to a Raspberry Pi to track hive variables like weight, temperature, sound, and visual data. A camera-based system at the hive entrance will count the incoming and outgoing bees and analyze their behavior using computer vision, contributing to the assessment of foraging efficiency and colony health. The research also includes investigating honeybee responses to Varroa destructor mites through an arena test, helping identify Varroa-resistant colonies and supporting future breeding programs.
